Doc Marten and the Flannels is the East Coast’s go-to tribute to the sound, energy, and attitude of 90s rock and grunge. Since forming in 2008, the band has played more than 500 shows, building a reputation for delivering a set that feels less like a cover show and more like stepping back into the peak of the decade.

Their performances tap into the raw edge and nostalgia of the era, earning reactions like “the most authentic grunge I’ve ever heard” and “if you close your eyes, you’d swear you were at a 90s concert.” It’s a show built for both die-hard fans who lived it the first time and newer audiences discovering the music for themselves.

Across Maryland and the broader Mid-Atlantic, Doc Marten and the Flannels have become a consistent crowd draw, regularly packing out venues like Twains Tavern, Cancun Cantina, Reckless Shepherd Brewing Co., and Purple Moose Saloon. Wherever they play, the result is the same: a full room, a loud crowd, and a setlist that keeps people singing along from start to finish.

The band features Billy Fisher on lead vocals, Chris Richard on guitar and backing vocals, Keith Thompson on bass and backing vocals, Steve Shaffer on guitar and backing vocals, and Anthony Grabowski on percussion. Together, they bring a level of musicianship and chemistry that elevates the show beyond nostalgia into a high-energy live experience.

Whether it’s a hometown bar, a packed festival crowd, or a beachside stage in Ocean City, Doc Marten and the Flannels deliver the kind of performance that reminds people why 90s rock still hits as hard as it ever did.
